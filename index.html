2. Research Gaps — Thesis Generators A & B

🔴 Gap A: Geometric & Morphological Ceilings

Some proteins (PD-1, PD-L1, Granzyme B) lack visual correlates in H&E — driven by cellular state, not morphology.

Information Gap:
H&E image X ∈ ℝH×W×3 → CODEX Y ∈ ℝH×W×40
HEX learns fθ: X → Ŷ minimizing
𝓛 = ‖Y − Ŷ‖²₂ + λ 𝓛SSIM
Ceiling: minf 𝔼[‖Y − f(X)‖²] — but X lacks
info about PTMs, signaling states, transient expression.
I(Y; X) is fundamentally bounded.

→ Thesis: Auxiliary features (nuclear morphology, chromatin texture) + uncertainty quantification.

🟠 Gap B: Out-of-Distribution Domain Shift

HEX trained on Stanford data — clinical deployment faces staining variability (20+ protocols), scanner differences (5+ manufacturers), and tissue artifacts.

Domain Shift:
Dtrain ~ Ptrain(X,Y), Dtest ~ Ptest(X,Y)
Ptrain ≠ Ptest
Δ = DKL(Ptrain ‖ Ptest)

Covariate shift: Ptrain(X) ≠ Ptest(X)
Concept shift: Ptrain(Y|X) ≠ Ptest(Y|X)

→ Thesis: Adversarial domain adaptation, stain normalization, test-time adaptation.

2. Research Gaps — Thesis Generators C & D

🟣 Gap C: Scalability of Multi-Modal Tokenization

HEX's 224×224 px patches miss global context: tumor-stroma ratios, long-range dependencies, multi-scale relationships. Cross-attention is O(N²) — intractable for ~200k patches per WSI.

Patch Count:
N = ⌊(H−p)/s + 1⌋ × ⌊(W−p)/s + 1⌋
For WSI at 20× (0.5 µm/px):
100k × 100k px → N ≈ 200,000 patches
Cross-attention: O(N²) ≈ 4×10¹⁰ — intractable
HEX likely uses MIL pooling → loses spatial structure.

→ Thesis: Hierarchical graph transformer with multi-scale tokens + efficient attention (linear, neighborhood sampling).

🩷 Gap D: Diagnostic Indeterminacy & Interpretability

HEX predicts proteins and outcomes — but is it learning true biology or spurious correlations? MICA improved prognosis by 22% and immunotherapy prediction by 24-39%, yet the why remains opaque.

Black Box Paradox:
• Model predicts proteins → clinicians can't verify without CODEX
• Causal vs. correlational — predicting immune markers from necrosis?
• Actionability gap: What confidence threshold justifies treatment change?

→ Thesis: Integrated Gradients, counterfactual explanations, causal discovery to disentangle biological signal from spurious correlation.

Thesis 1

Uncertainty-Aware Virtual Spatial Proteomics

Quantifying the Information Gap Between H&E Morphology and Protein Expression

Core Innovation: Model P(Y|X) as a distribution via:
• Conditional Diffusion Models
• Evidential Deep Learning (Dirichlet parameters)
• Deep Ensembles for variance estimation
Evidential Head: Predict (αi,j,k, βi,j,k) per pixel
𝓛 = ‖Y − Ŷ‖²₂ + λ DKL(Ppred ‖ Pprior)

Metrics: ECE, Selective Prediction AUROC, SSIM/PSNR for high-confidence regions

Thesis 2

Domain-Generalizable Virtual Spatial Proteomics

Closing the Clinical Translation Gap via Test-Time Adaptation

Core Innovation: 3-stage pipeline:
1. Train on source with domain-invariant features
2. Adapt to target with minimal paired data (5-10 WSIs)
3. Generalize across cancer types
Domain Confusion (GRL):
minθ maxφ 𝔼X~PS[log Dφ(fθ(X))]
+ 𝔼X~PT[log(1 − Dφ(fθ(X)))]
TTA: 𝓛adapt = H(Ŷ) + λ‖∇θH(Ŷ)‖²₂

Metrics: Pearson R on target, FHD, Domain Shift DKL, Generalization Gap

4.2 Bangladesh Feasibility Audit

❌ CODEX / Spatial Proteomics Infrastructure

Absent. No commercial CODEX, MIBI, or IMC platforms in Bangladesh. Akoya Biosciences, Ionpath, and Fluidigm have no local installations. Wet-lab spatial proteomics is not feasible within a 2-3 year thesis timeline.

⚠️ H&E Digital Pathology

Limited but growing. Some tertiary hospitals (BSMMU, Dhaka Medical College, NICRH) have digital scanners, but WSI archiving is not standardized.

✅ Computational Infrastructure

Adequate. GPU clusters at BUET, BRAC University, and via cloud (AWS, GCP, Azure). Model development, training, and evaluation are fully feasible with cloud GPU resources.

⚠️ Clinical Data & Ethics

Fragmented. No centralized cancer registry with linked H&E and outcomes. NICRH has paper-based records. BMRC ethics approval is established.

✅ Verdict: This research CAN be conducted in Bangladesh — but exclusively using open-source, publicly available datasets. Local wet-lab spatial proteomics is not feasible. Computational components are fully feasible with cloud GPU resources.

Risk Mitigation: (1) Collaborate with international partners (Stanford, Yale, MD Anderson) for data access. (2) Use pre-processed datasets from public repositories. (3) Focus on computational innovation, not experimental data generation. (4) Consider spatial transcriptomics (10x Visium) as a proxy — more publicly available than CODEX.
